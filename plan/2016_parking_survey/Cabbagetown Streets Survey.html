    Cabbageheads Only - Residential Parking Zone

    Neighbors and visitors coming together in Cabbagetown is a great thing, but until Atlanta becomes a fully connected transit-filled metropolis, many of us need a place to park our cars on the street when we get home at night. In order to implement a Residential Parking Zone, a *much* larger effort would be undertaken by neighbors and the city, and in order to begin, a petition signed by at least 70% of residents on any contiguous block (or blocks) must first be submitted to the city to initiate a comprehensive study. If qualified, approved, and created, the zone would restrict parking by non-residents either completely or with a two-hour limit, for a twelve-hour period (such as 12 noon to 12 midnight). Zones are patrolled and enforced by ParkAtlanta. Residents may purchase a permit costing $20/year to park a vehicle on the street in the zone. Residents without private off-street parking may purchase up to two permits, while residents who do have off-street parking may purchase one permit. All residents may obtain, free of charge, up two "visitors passes" to be used by guests for up to 15 days. You may read more about the process for creating and enforcing a residential parking zone here: https://www.municode.com/library/ga/atlanta/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=COORATGEVOII_CH150TRVE_ARTIVSTSTPA_DIV4REPEPA All that said, how supportive would you be of the following action? 1=Least 2=Less 3=Neutral 4=More 5=Most
